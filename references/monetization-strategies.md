# Monetization Strategies for Indie Games

## Framework: Choose by Platform + Audience

| Strategy | Best for | Platform | Risk |
|----------|----------|----------|------|
| Premium (one-time purchase) | Niche, quality-focused | Steam, App Store | Low |
| Free + ethical IAP | Casual, high retention | Mobile | Medium |
| Freemium + premium unlock | Wide funnel, engaged core | Mobile + Steam | Medium |
| DLC / expansion packs | Established fanbase | Steam | Low |
| Subscription | Content-heavy, live service | Mobile | High |
| Early Access | Community-funded dev | Steam | Medium |
| Kickstarter / crowdfunding | Pre-launch community | Any | Variable |
| Ad-supported (F2P) | Hyper-casual | Mobile only | High (scale needed) |

---

## Strategy Details

### Premium (One-Time Purchase)

**How it works:** Players pay once, own the game forever.

**Best conditions:**
- Game has clear, complete experience (beginning, middle, end)
- Target audience is willing to research before buying
- Strong word-of-mouth potential (quality over quantity)

**Pricing guidance:**
- Mobile: $2.99–$6.99 (higher = lower conversion, but higher value perception)
- Steam: $4.99–$14.99 for indie, $15–$30 for mid-size indie

**Small-team advantages:**
- No live ops required
- Revenue is front-loaded at launch
- No ongoing content obligation

**Pitfall:** Requires effective launch marketing — most revenue comes in the first 2 weeks.

---

### Free + Ethical IAP (In-App Purchases)

**How it works:** Game is free; players buy cosmetics, content, or convenience.

**Ethical IAP** (what indie audiences accept):
- Cosmetics only (skins, themes, character customization)
- Content packs (new levels, scenarios, story chapters)
- Permanent convenience (ad removal, expanded storage)
- "Tip jar" or "supporter" packs — no gameplay advantage

**Avoid:** Energy systems, paywall progression gates, loot boxes

**Best for:** Casual mobile games with daily engagement loop

**Revenue reality:** Top 5% of players generate 80%+ of IAP revenue. Need volume.

---

### Freemium + Premium Unlock

**How it works:** Core game is free, full version requires one-time unlock purchase.

**Best conditions:**
- Game has a "hook" that's addictive in the free tier
- Full version adds meaningful depth (not just more of the same)
- Can be positioned as "try before you buy"

**Example:** Puzzle game — first 30 levels free, buy full pack for $3.99

**Small-team advantages:**
- Low conversion (5–15%) but qualified buyers
- App Store/Steam algorithms often favor free apps for discovery

---

### DLC / Expansion Packs

**How it works:** Base game + optional paid add-ons

**Best conditions:**
- Existing fanbase who wants more content
- Game has modular content structure (level packs, story chapters, campaigns)

**Pricing:** DLC typically 30–50% of base game price

**Timing:** Don't announce DLC before launch — focus on making the base game great

---

### Early Access (Steam)

**How it works:** Ship unfinished game at reduced price, update over months

**Best conditions:**
- Game already has core loop working
- Developer is comfortable with public feedback during development
- Genre where community input improves the game (roguelikes, survival, management sims)

**Pricing:** 20–40% below planned final price

**Pitfall:** Early Access fatigue — many games never leave EA. Have a clear exit plan.

---

### Crowdfunding (Kickstarter / Patreon)

**How it works:** Collect funds from supporters before or during development

**Best for:** Games with strong conceptual appeal (narrative, art style, niche genre)

**Realistic targets for indie:** $5,000–$50,000

**Patreon:** Better for long-term, content-driven (devlogs, behind-the-scenes)

## Platform-Specific Notes

### Mobile App Store (iOS / Android)
- Discovery is harder than Steam — requires ASO (App Store Optimization)
- Apple's 30% cut; Google's 15% for first $1M
- Free apps get 50–100x more downloads than paid apps at same quality
- Reviews are critical — prompt for reviews at the right moment

### Steam
- 30% cut, but better discovery tools (tags, recommendations, wishlists)
- Wishlists are the key metric — aim for 5,000+ before launch
- Steam Next Fest (2x/year) is major exposure opportunity for demos
- Refund policy (2 hrs / 14 days) — design accordingly

### Cross-Platform (Mobile + Steam)
- Can work well for turn-based, casual, or management games
- Price anchor effect: $14.99 on Steam makes $4.99 mobile look accessible
- Different monetization per platform is acceptable (premium mobile, F2P mobile)
