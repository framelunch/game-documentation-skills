# 5-Axis Game Idea Evaluation Rubric

Each axis is scored 1–5. Higher is better. Use ⭐ symbols in the report.

---

## Axis 1: Feasibility (実現可能性)

**Question:** Can a solo developer or small team (1–3 people) actually build this game?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 5 | Core mechanics are simple and proven. Can ship MVP in < 2 months. Standard tech (Godot/Unity). No original 3D art required. |
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐ 4 | Straightforward to build. MVP in 2–4 months. Minor technical challenges. 2D art or procedural art feasible. |
| ⭐⭐⭐ 3 | Moderate complexity. MVP in 4–6 months. Requires solving at least one hard technical problem. |
| ⭐⭐ 2 | Complex. MVP in 6–12 months. Requires specialized skills (networking, 3D, AI/ML). |
| ⭐ 1 | Very hard. Requires large team, unproven tech, or massive asset production. Not realistic for solo dev. |

**Key questions:**
- Can I describe the MVP in one paragraph?
- Does this require multiplayer servers, physics simulation, or real-time networking?
- Is the art style achievable solo (pixel art, minimalist, procedural)?

---

## Axis 2: Development Timeline (開発期間)

**Question:** How quickly can this reach a shippable state?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 5 | MVP in < 1 month. Can be built with existing game jam code or templates. |
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐ 4 | MVP in 1–3 months. Clear scope, minimal unknowns. |
| ⭐⭐⭐ 3 | MVP in 3–6 months. Some scope uncertainty but manageable. |
| ⭐⭐ 2 | MVP in 6–12 months. High scope risk, likely to expand during development. |
| ⭐ 1 | > 12 months to a shippable product. High risk of never launching. |

**Key questions:**
- Is the feature list fixed, or will it grow during development?
- Are there dependencies on third-party systems, licensed music, voice acting?
- Is this a "once I have 50% of it done, it's playable" game or an "all-or-nothing" game?

---

## Axis 3: Profitability (収益性)

**Question:** What is the realistic revenue potential based on comparable games and market evidence?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 5 | Strong comparable games earning $10k+ at launch. Clear path to $1k+ MRR. Indie Hackers evidence exists. |
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐ 4 | Comparable games earned $2k–$10k. Market is underserved, pricing power exists. |
| ⭐⭐⭐ 3 | Some comparable success stories. Revenue likely $500–$2k. Good for portfolio, not primary income. |
| ⭐⭐ 2 | Limited evidence. Revenue likely < $500. Market exists but monetization is unclear. |
| ⭐ 1 | Saturated market. Dominant free competitors. No clear monetization path. |

**Key questions:**
- What do the top 3 comparable games earn on Steam/App Store?
- Is this genre typically premium or F2P?
- Are there Indie Hackers success stories in this niche?

---

## Axis 4: Competitive Advantage (競合優位性)

**Question:** Does this game have something that sets it apart from existing alternatives?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 5 | Fills a clear gap no existing game covers. Unique mechanic combination. |
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐ 4 | Meaningfully better or different from existing options. Clear differentiation statement. |
| ⭐⭐⭐ 3 | Solid execution of an existing formula. "X genre done right" or "X genre on mobile". |
| ⭐⭐ 2 | Minor differentiation. Would compete head-to-head with established games on execution quality alone. |
| ⭐ 1 | Direct clone of a popular game with no meaningful differentiator. |

**Key questions:**
- Can I complete the sentence "This is the only game that..."?
- Is the gap coming from platform (no mobile version), mechanic (no one combined X + Y), or audience (underserved demographic)?
- Would players switch from an existing favorite, or is this their only option?

---

## Axis 5: Small-Team Suitability (小規模開発適性)

**Question:** Is this game type well-suited for a solo developer or 2–3 person team, specifically?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 5 | Ideal for solo: minimal art/music assets, procedural content, no live ops, text-heavy or minimalist visuals. |
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐ 4 | Good for small team: modest art budget, no server infrastructure, 2D or simple 3D. |
| ⭐⭐⭐ 3 | Manageable: requires outsourcing 1–2 skills (music, art) but core is doable. |
| ⭐⭐ 2 | Challenging: requires continuous content updates, server infrastructure, or extensive art. |
| ⭐ 1 | Poor fit: needs full team (programmer, artist, designer, marketing, community manager, live ops). |

**Key questions:**
- Can one person be the lead developer and still ship in a reasonable time?
- Does the game require ongoing content (new levels, events, seasonal updates)?
- Is server infrastructure required (matchmaking, leaderboards, multiplayer)?
- Can asset requirements be met with royalty-free music and AI-assisted or purchased art?

---

## Composite Score Interpretation

Sum all five scores (max 25):

| Total | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| 20–25 | **Highly recommended** — Strong fit for indie development, validated market |
| 15–19 | **Recommended with caveats** — Good opportunity with manageable risks |
| 10–14 | **Conditional** — Possible but requires specific conditions or skills |
| 5–9 | **Not recommended** — High risk, low reward for small teams |

---

## Scoring Notes

- These scores reflect **small-team feasibility**, not game quality potential
- A 5/5 on all axes is rare — most strong opportunities score 3–4 across the board
- Weight Feasibility and Small-Team Suitability highest for solo developers
- Weight Profitability and Competitive Advantage highest for developers with prior experience
