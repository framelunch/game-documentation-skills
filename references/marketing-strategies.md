# Marketing Strategies for Indie Games

## Core Principle: Start Marketing Before You're Ready

The biggest indie marketing mistake is "I'll figure out marketing when the game is done."
Marketing for indie games is about building an audience over 12–24 months, not a
2-week launch blitz. Start Day 1 of development.

---

## Channel Overview

| Channel | Cost | Time to Results | Best For |
|---------|------|-----------------|----------|
| Reddit | Free | 1–7 days | Niche validation, early community |
| TikTok / YouTube Shorts | Free + time | 1–4 weeks | Visual, casual games |
| Twitter/X devlog | Free + time | Months | Indie dev community |
| Steam Next Fest | Free | 1–2 weeks | Steam wishlist building |
| Press/influencer outreach | Free + effort | 2–4 weeks | Mid-size launch |
| Paid ads (Facebook/Google) | $ | Immediate | Mobile F2P at scale |
| App Store Optimization (ASO) | Free | Weeks–months | Mobile discovery |
| Discord community | Free | Months | Retention + loyal fans |

## Strategy by Phase

### Phase 1: Pre-development (concept validation)

**Goal:** Validate demand before writing a single line of code.

- Post the concept on relevant subreddits: "Would you play a game like X?"
- Share mockups on TikTok or Twitter — gauge reaction
- Search Reddit/HN for pain points that your game would solve
- Set up a "coming soon" landing page with email capture

**Target:** 50+ genuine "I would play this" responses = proceed with confidence

---

### Phase 2: Development (audience building)

**Goal:** Build an email list and community before launch.

**Devlog strategy (Twitter/X or Bluesky):**
- Post progress 2–3x per week
- Show satisfying GIFs of game mechanics
- Use hashtags: `#gamedev`, `#indiedev`, `#screenshotsaturday`, `#godot`, `#unity`
- Engage with other indie devs — the community is generous with shares

**TikTok / YouTube Shorts:**
- "I'm making a game that does X" — hook content works well
- Show before/after of art, mechanics, UI improvements
- Even a modest viral post can drive thousands of wishlists

**Reddit strategy:**
- Post devlogs in `r/indiegaming`, `r/gamedev`, `r/IndieDev`
- Don't just post and leave — respond to every comment
- Share on genre-specific subreddits (e.g., `r/cozygames` for cozy games)
- Rule: post updates, not ads. Players want to follow a journey.

**Steam wishlist (if Steam game):**
- Set up Steam page as early as possible (even with placeholder content)
- Wishlists = launch day sales estimate
- Each Next Fest can add 1,000–10,000 wishlists

---

### Phase 3: Pre-launch (30–60 days out)

**Goal:** Maximize launch day traffic.

**Press outreach:**
- Target indie game journalists: TouchArcade, Pocket Tactics (mobile), PC Gamer (Steam)
- Also target YouTubers / streamers in your game's genre (100k–1M subs sweet spot)
- Send personalized pitch emails with: a key image, a 30-second GIF, a download link
- Do this 4–6 weeks before launch

**Community activation:**
- Host a "closed beta" on Discord — 50–200 engaged testers are worth 10,000 passive followers
- Ask beta testers to leave reviews/ratings on day 1 of public launch

**Trailer:**
- 60–90 second trailer showing actual gameplay within the first 10 seconds
- Post on YouTube, TikTok, Twitter, Reddit simultaneously

---

### Phase 4: Launch week

**Goal:** Maximize visibility during algorithm-boosted launch window.

- Post on every channel simultaneously on launch day
- Ask friends/family/community to leave reviews (mobile) or purchase (Steam)
- Monitor and respond to all reviews in the first 48 hours
- Post a "game is live!" thread on relevant subreddits (check community rules first)
- Thank early players publicly — it generates goodwill and engagement

## Platform-Specific Tactics

### Mobile (iOS / Android)

**App Store Optimization (ASO):**
- Research keywords with tools like AppFollow, Sensor Tower (free tiers available)
- Main keyword in the app name (30 character limit)
- First 3 lines of description must hook — most users don't click "more"
- Screenshots are your storefront — design them like ads, not gameplay captures

**Cross-promotion:**
- Partner with other indie devs to feature each other's games
- Especially effective between games with similar audiences (same genre or play style)

**Retention tactics that drive word-of-mouth:**
- Daily rewards, streak systems — keep players coming back
- "Refer a friend" mechanics if game has social element

### Steam

**Tags are everything:**
- Add all relevant genre tags immediately on setup
- Wrong tags = wrong audience = poor reviews

**Steam Next Fest:**
- Submit a demo 2+ months before fest
- Goal: 1,000 wishlists during fest = good launch momentum
- Monitor and respond to all community hub posts during fest

**Post-launch updates:**
- Steam heavily rewards games with regular updates (algorithm boosts)
- Even small quality-of-life updates generate "update" news posts
- Plan 1–2 updates per month for 6 months post-launch

## Marketing on a Budget: Priority Order

If you have zero marketing budget, do these in order:

1. **Build in public on Twitter/X** — free, compounds over time
2. **Post on relevant subreddits** — free, immediate community feedback
3. **TikTok devlogs** — algorithm rewards interesting content regardless of follower count
4. **Steam page early** — wishlists are free and compound over time
5. **Discord community** — build your core fans, they'll do marketing for you
6. **Press outreach** — free but time-intensive; batch-contact 20 outlets at once
